# Abstract

This poster presents a pilot study on the syntactic organization of knowledge in Tang dynasty regulated fu (lüfu). It focuses on how classical allusions are not merely accumulated as philological references, but reorganized through recurrent syntactic frames.

Using annotated examples from Li Cheng’s 李程 lüfu works, the project combines TEI-XML encoding, syntactic classification based on fu-pu categories, and allusion analysis. The study proposes that syntactic structures function as mediating frames through which inherited knowledge is selected, transformed, and converted into propositions or judgments.

The project contributes to digital humanities approaches to classical Chinese literature by linking TEI-based annotation, syntactic modeling, and the analysis of intertextual knowledge networks.
